# Chapter 1 – Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

This chapter provides an overview of Artificial Intelligence (AI), including its definition, historical development, and major research areas. It establishes the fundamental concepts required for understanding intelligent systems and AI problem-solving approaches.

---

## 1. What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence is the field of computer science focused on building intelligent systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.

There are four main perspectives of AI:

### 1. Acting Humanly

* Systems behave like humans
* Based on the **Turing Test**
* Requires:

  * Natural Language Processing
  * Knowledge Representation
  * Automated Reasoning
  * Machine Learning

### 2. Thinking Humanly

* Systems simulate human thinking
* Based on cognitive modeling
* Uses psychology and cognitive science

### 3. Thinking Rationally

* Systems use logical reasoning
* Based on formal logic
* Example:

  * All men are mortal
  * Socrates is a man
  * Therefore Socrates is mortal

### 4. Acting Rationally

* Systems act to achieve the best outcome
* Based on rational agents
* Most modern AI systems follow this approach

Artificial Intelligence is built upon multiple disciplines:

* Philosophy
* Mathematics
* Economics
* Neuroscience
* Psychology

* Control Theory
* Linguistics

These fields contribute to reasoning, learning, perception, and decision-making.

## 3. History of Artificial Intelligence

### Early Period (1950s–1960s)

* Game playing
* Theorem proving
* Early neural networks

### Symbolic AI (1970s)

* Expert systems
* Knowledge-based systems

### Commercial Period (1980s)

* Rule-based systems
* Industrial applications

### Modern AI (1990s–Present)

* Machine learning
* Data-driven AI
* Deep learning
* Big data applications
